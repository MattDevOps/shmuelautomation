Publishing a listing to Facebook

A simple, step-by-step guide. Print it or keep it open while you post.

← Back to the full admin guide

What this does (and what it doesn't)

The system writes your Facebook post for you — the listing's text, price, and details, in Hebrew and English. It puts that text on your clipboard and points you straight at your Facebook group. You then paste it, add the photos, and press Post.

Why you still press Post yourself: Facebook does not allow apps to auto-post into groups — accounts that do it get blocked. So the system does the slow part (writing and formatting the post) and leaves the final tap to you. Think of it as a one-tap helper, not a robot. This keeps your Facebook account safe.

One-time setup: add your Facebook groups

Do this once. After that, every post takes seconds.

  1. Open the admin and go to the Groups page.
  2. Click Add group.
  3. Fill in:
    • Name — e.g. "Jerusalem Apartments for Rent".
    • Platform — choose Facebook.
    • Audience — Rent, Sale, or Both. (A rental listing will then only show your rental groups, so you never post a rental into a buyers-only group.)
    • Target URL — open the group in your browser, copy the address from the top bar, and paste it here. This is the link the share screen will open for you.
  4. Save. Repeat for each Facebook group you post to.
Tip: add every group you use — buyers, renters, neighborhood groups. The more you set up now, the less typing later.

Posting a listing — step by step

Once your groups are set up, this is the everyday flow.

1

Open the listing you want to post

Two easy ways:

  • Open the Queue page — it shows you which listings are due to post right now, so you don't have to think about what to share.
  • Or open the Properties page and click the listing you have in mind.
2

Click "Compose & share"

A share box opens. It already contains the finished post — a clean write-up of the apartment with price, rooms, neighborhood, and size, in both Hebrew and English.

3

Read it over (and edit if you like)

The text is ready to go, but it's yours — feel free to tweak a word, add a line, or drop in a hashtag before you copy it.

4

Click "Copy text"

The whole post is now on your clipboard, ready to paste. (You'll paste it into Facebook in a moment.)

5

Grab the photos

Open the listing's photos (from the property's photo section) and save the ones you want to post — usually 3 to 6 of the best shots. Facebook posts with photos get far more views than text alone.

6

Open your Facebook group

In the share box, click the Facebook group you want to post to. It opens that group in a new tab. Start a new post there.

7

Paste, add photos, and Post

Paste the text (Ctrl + V, or ⌘ + V on a Mac), attach the photos you saved, and press Facebook's Post button. That's it — the listing is live in that group.

Repeat steps 6–7 for each group you want to share it in. The text is still on your clipboard, so you can paste it again and again.

8

Mark it posted in the admin

Come back to the share box and click Mark slot as posted. This tells the system you've shared this one, so it moves on and suggests the next listing next time — you'll never lose track or double-post.

Good habits

  • Post at the busy times. The Queue suggests two windows a day (morning and evening) when groups are most active.
  • Shabbat is handled for you. The system won't suggest posts from Friday afternoon until Saturday night, so you don't have to remember.
  • Always include photos. A listing with photos gets many more replies than one without.
  • Don't spam one group. Posting the same apartment into the same group several times a day can get you muted or removed. Once per posting window is plenty.

If something doesn't work

  • "Copy text" didn't seem to do anything. It copies silently — just go to Facebook and paste. If nothing pastes, select the text in the box manually and copy it the normal way.
  • The group link opens the wrong place. Go to the Groups page, open the group, and re-paste its correct web address into the Target URL field.
  • I don't see my Facebook group in the share box. Either it isn't added yet (add it on the Groups page) or its audience doesn't match this listing — e.g. a rental listing won't show a sale-only group. Check the group's audience setting.
